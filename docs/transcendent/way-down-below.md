---

## Thematic Arc

The five hooks form a complete emotional journey in miniature:

1. **Descent** — "way down below" places the speaker in a low, interior space. Below what? Below pride, below surface. Could be underground, could be depth of feeling.
2. **Tenderness exposed** — warmth declared before defenses go up. Raw.
3. **Overwhelm through absurdity** — "melts like a blender" is surreal, almost comic. The feeling is so total it breaks metaphor. This is the hook that will stick.
4. **The wound** — "need a mender" — someone or something to put the speaker back together.
5. **The prayer through the instrument** — "Lord let them hear my Fender" elevates the guitar from tool to vessel. The Fender becomes the voice that says what words can't. This is the spiritual punch.

**Core tension:** Broken person, warm heart, asking God to let the *guitar* speak on their behalf.

## Possible Structure Directions

- **Verse → Hook cascade:** Each hook lands on its own line with space before it — let the groove breathe.
- **Call and response:** Verses describe the world/scene; hooks are the confession underneath.
- **Single-hook refrain:** Pick the strongest hook as the chorus nucleus — likely *"Lord let them hear my Fender"* — and cascade the others as pre-chorus build.
